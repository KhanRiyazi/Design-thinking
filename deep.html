Design Thinking Process

The foundation of human-centered design starts with deep understanding and empathy

About This Phase

This stage is the foundation of the design thinking process. It's all about observing, listening, and asking the right questions to deeply understand the people and problems we're designing for.

We immerse ourselves in the user's world to gain insights that data alone can't provide, uncovering latent needs and emotional drivers.

This phase is all about asking the right questions and listening deeply to uncover insights that will guide the entire project.

Methods Used

  • User Interviews & Surveys
  • Market Analysis
  • Stakeholder Interviews
  • Empathy Mapping
  • Contextual Observation
  • Journey Mapping
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Data Synthesis

Key Outcomes

By the end of this phase, we generate insights that define the problem space clearly, ensuring the project moves forward with a human-centered direction.
